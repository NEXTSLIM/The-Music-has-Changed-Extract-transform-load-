The rise and fall of music formats over the last half-century for vinyl records, 8-track tapes, cassette tapes, CDs, digital downloads, and now streaming music are good examples of the economic concept of “creative destruction” in the recorded music business. According to the economist Joseph Schumpeter the “gales of creative destruction” describe the “processes of industrial mutation that continuously revolutionize the economic structure from within, incessantly destroying the old ones, incessantly creating new ones.” Physical music formats (LPs, tapes, CDs) have been “destroyed” and have now pretty much all been replaced with streaming music.
